Welcome to Basilar

A free task app with the bare minimum of functionality

4 tasks

"What three things do you need to do today?

- Jeff Atwood

Basilar is the app and service that allows you to plan for completing 3 tasks a day, and only 3 tasks.
This app will help you plan and remember those 3 tasks, and allow you to schedule the coming week.

Time

Time is something different for every individual. Some thinks there is never enough time, it keeps coming more and more of it. Others don't think they have enough. Some thinks time equals money, while others want to have as much leasure as possible.

What you don't want, is waste time planning how to use the available time.
Your mind already knows a lot of tasks ahead, and it's capable of planning well into the future, even without your awareness.

So use Basilar as a tool to skip detailed planning. with those lengthy processes and complex software, and instead focus on what you can accomplish each and every day.

Fast

Basilar is fast to use and understand. You can in a very short time, plan for today and the coming week.

Fast, lean, bare.

Cloud

Basilar is always in sync across all your devices, making your tasks accessible on your computers or phones. It work across all devices and platforms.

Why you should not use To-Do lists

Adam Wozniak have written an excellent article on the pitfalls and issues with the regular To-Do lists.

What are the reasons he provides?

  • Lists give the illusion of progress
  • Lists give the illusion of accomplishment
  • Lists make you feel guilty for not achieving these things
  • Lists make you feel guilty for continually delaying certain items
  • Lists make you feel guilty for not doing things you don’t want to be doing anyway
  • Lists make you prioritise the wrong things
  • Lists are inefficient (think of what you could do with all the time you spend maintaining your lists?)
  • Lists suck the enjoyment out of activities, making most things feel like an obligation
  • Lists don’t actually make you more organised long term
  • Lists can close you off to spontaneity and exploration of things you didn’t plan for (let’s face it, it’s impossible to REALLY plan some things in life)
